01 — Channel
Amazon
Nov 2025 – Apr 2026
Source: Amazon Business Report
Summary Insights
01

Inventory stockouts caused a major revenue dip. A shortage of Belly Fat Burner and Muscle Builder from ~Oct 2025 drove revenue down to a low of $63.7K in February — a 61% decline from November's $162.1K peak. Revenue recovered strongly to $144.8K in March upon restocking.

02

Belly Fat Burner and Muscle Builder dominate revenue. In healthy months (Nov, Dec, Apr), these two SKUs together account for roughly 75–80% of total Amazon revenue, making inventory continuity for these products business-critical.

03

Subscription base is declining. The Subscribe & Save count dropped from 4,750 in November to 3,730 in April — a 21% decline over six months. Out-of-stock periods accelerated cancellations, with OOS lost sales peaking at $7,130 in March.

04

Product insert lead gen is improving. The scan-to-order rate grew from 6.4% to 8.5% (Nov → Apr), and email capture is trending up — 143 emails captured in April vs. 56 in February. The funnel is gaining efficiency even as order volume fluctuates.

05

GLP-1 Support showed a rapid rise and abrupt exit. The product went from $18.7K in February to $43.8K in March, then dropped to just $2.5K in April — suggesting another stockout or product discontinuation requiring monitoring.

02 — Channel
Shopify
Apr 2025 – Apr 2026
Source: Shopify Analytics
Summary Insights
01

October 2025 was the channel's strongest month, with $13,111 in sales and 319 orders. This coincides with a surge in sessions (10,930) and the highest share of one-time purchases (44%), suggesting effective traffic acquisition that month.

02

Subscriptions are the backbone of Shopify revenue. In most months, 60–78% of all orders are subscriptions, meaning the channel's revenue stability is heavily dependent on retention, not acquisition.

03

Conversion rate collapsed during high-traffic periods. When sessions surged to 9,202–12,923 (Jul–Dec), conversion rates dropped to 0.41%–0.76% — far below the 2.65–2.71% seen in lower-traffic months (Apr–May). Traffic quality or landing page fit may be a factor.

04

Average order value has grown significantly. AOV rose from $22.53 in May 2025 to $44.99 in Jan 2026 — nearly doubling — suggesting effective upselling, bundle adoption, or product mix shift toward higher-priced SKUs.

05

Traffic dropped sharply in early 2026. Sessions fell from a peak of 12,923 (Dec) to just 2,460 by April 2026, likely tied to the wind-down of Google Ads. Shopify's fate is closely coupled to paid traffic investment.

03 — Channel
Email Marketing
Jul 2025 – Apr 2026
Source: MailChimp
Summary Insights
01

The list was dramatically pruned in November 2025. Total contacts fell from 20,687 to 5,059 after inactive contacts were archived. This explains the sharp drop in sends and likely reflects a move toward a more engaged, deliverable-focused list.

02

Open rates are healthy; click rates are critically low. MailChimp flags open rates as above industry average, but click rates sit at 0.12–0.36% against sends — pointing to weak CTAs, misaligned content, or emails that fail to drive action beyond opens.

03

Revenue from email is minimal relative to send volume. Despite 49,565–257,015 monthly sends, revenue generated is only $563–$1,147/month. Revenue per recipient consistently sits at $0.01, indicating the channel has very limited direct conversion impact.

04

Engagement is declining as the list matures. Both open rate (7.51% → 6.23%) and absolute opens (5,874 → 3,081) have trended downward from Jan → Apr 2026, suggesting list fatigue or send frequency may need revisiting.

05

Average order value from email is competitive. Email-driven AOV of $28–$57 is in line with or above Shopify's AOV, suggesting that when email does convert, the buyer quality is strong — the challenge is driving enough of those conversions.

Funnel: Amazon Order → Insert Scan → Email Capture → App Sign-Up  ·  Rates shown are 6-month averages (Nov 2025 – Apr 2026)

Summary Insights

01

Scan rate is improving despite volume fluctuations. The share of Amazon orders that result in a QR scan grew from 6.4% in November to 8.5% in April, even as order volumes swung significantly. The insert itself is gaining traction as customers become more familiar with the journey.

02

Email capture from scans is strong but softening. The email-capture-to-scan rate peaked at 58.1% in January and has since declined to 42.8% in April. This warrants a review of the questionnaire's end CTA to re-sharpen the opt-in incentive.

03

App sign-up rate is the biggest drop-off point. On average, only 27.7% of scans convert to an app sign-up — roughly half the email capture rate. The step from "email submitted" to "app downloaded" represents the largest attrition in the funnel and is the highest-leverage area for optimisation.

04

Absolute lead volume is recovering with order volume. Email captures bottomed at 56 in February during the stockout period and rebounded to 143 in April — the highest of any month. App sign-ups followed the same trajectory, reaching 78 in April, also a 6-month high.

Automated Email Sequences

Summary Insights

01

Abandoned cart emails have never generated a completed order. Across all 12 months of data (May 2025 – Apr 2026), zero orders were recovered from abandoned cart sends. Despite reasonable open rates (up to 90% in April), clicks are sporadic and no send period has converted. The sequence triggers correctly but is failing to close.

02

Abandoned cart open rates are volatile and trending up. After a poor patch in August–October (13–23%), open rates have recovered sharply — reaching 67% in March and 90% in April. This suggests deliverability and subject line performance have improved, but the email body or offer is not compelling enough to drive a click-through to purchase.

03

Refill Reminder #2 is outperforming Reminder #1 on conversions. Despite fewer sends, Refill Reminder #2 generated 6 orders vs. 3 orders for Reminder #1 across March–April, with an Orders/Clicks rate of 100% in both months — meaning every person who clicked bought. The second reminder appears to reach customers at a higher-intent moment.

04

Refill Reminder revenue is growing quickly. Combined revenue from both reminders grew from $119 in March to $308 in April — a 159% increase in a single month. With the sequences only live since March 12, the revenue potential as the subscriber base grows is significant.

05

Open-to-click rates on refill reminders are low. Reminder #1 sees only 14–18% of openers clicking, and Reminder #2 fares slightly better at 31–33%. Given that 100% of those who do click go on to purchase, the biggest opportunity is converting more openers into clickers — stronger CTAs, clearer urgency, or a discount incentive could move the needle meaningfully.

04 — Channel
Google Ads
Jul 2025 – Feb 2026
Source: Google Ads Dashboard
Summary Insights
01

ROAS never exceeded breakeven for most of the period. ROAS peaked at 1.34 in October 2025 — the only month spend was technically profitable. Before and after, ROAS ranged from 0.31–0.96, meaning the channel was consistently spending more than it returned.

02

October was the clear performance peak. Cost/conversion dropped to a record low of $38.60 with 157.62 conversions — the highest of any month. Both ROAS and conversion volume confirmed this as the period's best-performing month.

03

December–February saw severe performance deterioration. ROAS collapsed to 0.39 and 0.31 respectively, driven by inventory shortages on the hero product (Belly Fat Burner). Cost per conversion ballooned to $128–$134, making every sale deeply unprofitable.

04

Impression volume was high but click quality was inconsistent. Despite 118K–1.02M monthly impressions, CTR ranged from 0.85% to 2.18% with no clear trend. The September low (0.85% CTR on 679K impressions) points to a mismatch between ad creative and audience targeting.

05

The channel was shut down before inventory recovered. The Google Ads agency relationship was terminated by the time stock returned in mid-March 2026, ending the data series. The channel's full recovery potential during restock was never measured.

05 — Initiative Updates
What We're Working On
As of May 2026
Recent & Current Initiatives
Recently Completed
Website & Product Page Renovation

We completed a full rebuild of our direct-to-consumer website and all corresponding product pages. This was a significant undertaking with two core objectives:

  • Brand realignment. All content was rewritten and redesigned to reflect our updated brand direction — "Sustainable weight loss for parents" — ensuring messaging consistency across every product touchpoint.
  • Higher average order value. We restructured our product offers to make the 3-month supply the default option, which gives customers greater savings per unit and positions multi-month commitments as the natural choice rather than the premium ask.
  • App-linked incentive for subscribers. Subscription orders on the 3-month supply now include free access to the UNALTERED App — creating a bundled value proposition that rewards commitment and differentiates the subscription tier from one-time purchases.
In Progress
Website × UNALTERED App Integration

We are currently building a direct integration between our Shopify website and the UNALTERED App, creating a unified commerce and wellness experience for our customers.

  • In-app purchasing. App users will be able to order products and manage their supplement subscriptions directly inside the app — removing friction between a customer's health tracking experience and their purchasing workflow.
  • Subscription-gated app access. Active subscription members will receive free app access as part of their membership, reinforcing the value of staying subscribed. Non-subscribers will have access to the app on a paid monthly basis, creating an independent revenue stream for the app itself.
  • Strategic intent. This integration positions the UNALTERED App as the central accountability layer for our customers' health journey — deepening retention, reducing churn, and tying product usage to daily habit tracking.
